# American University of Science and Technology (AUST)

## Overview

- **Founded:** 1989 (by Dr. Hiam Sakr as an external degree program of SUNY Empire State College; initially "American Universal College")
- **Type:** Private, non-sectarian, co-educational university
- **Education Type:** American model (credit-based)
- **Campus(es):**
  - **Main campus:** Ashrafieh, Beirut
  - **Zahlé campus** (Bekaa)
  - **Sidon campus** (South Lebanon)
  - **Bhamdoun campus** (Mount Lebanon)
- **Language of instruction:** English (primary), with French and Arabic for select programs
- **Websites:**
  - Main: [aust.edu.lb](https://www.aust.edu.lb/)

AUST was founded in 1989 by Dr. Hiam Sakr, initially as an external degree program of
the State University of New York (SUNY) Empire State College. It operates four campuses
across Lebanon and serves approximately **5,522 students** (5,098 undergraduate, 414
graduate). AUST has 33+ international university partnerships across North America,
Europe, Asia, and Africa. Engineering and Computer Science programs are **ABET
accredited** (since 2016), business programs are **IACBE accredited** (since 2011), and
the institution holds **EVALAG institutional accreditation** (since 2018).

AUST organizes its programs across 4 faculties. Programs follow the American credit
system (~30 credits/year). Most bachelors are **3–4 years** (105–118 credits);
engineering is **4 years** (118 credits). Years below are derived from credits.

## Application

### Application fees

- **Application fee:** $25 (non-refundable)

### Requirements

#### SAT

AUST accepts SAT scores per MEHE freshman minimums (870 Arts / 950 Sciences). Applicants
without SAT take the AUST entrance exam.

> TODO: Per-faculty SAT minimums for direct admission (beyond freshman) not published.

#### English proficiency

All instruction is in English. Applicants demonstrate proficiency via TOEFL, IELTS, SAT
Reading/Writing, or the AUST English placement exam.

> TODO: Specific TOEFL/IELTS minimum thresholds not published publicly.

#### High-school grades

- Lebanese Baccalaureate or equivalent
- Equivalence from the Lebanese Ministry of Education for non-Lebanese curricula

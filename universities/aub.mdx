# American University of Beirut (AUB)

## Overview

- **Founded:** 1866
- **Type:** Private, not-for-profit
- **Education Type:** American liberal arts model
- **Campus(es):** Beirut (main campus + AUB Medical Center)
- **Language of instruction:** English
- **Websites:**
  - Main: [aub.edu.lb](https://www.aub.edu.lb/)
  - Admissions: [aub.edu.lb/admissions](https://www.aub.edu.lb/admissions/Pages/default.aspx)
  - Financial Aid: [aub.edu.lb/faid](https://www.aub.edu.lb/faid/Pages/default.aspx)
  - Apply online: [join.aub.edu.lb/apply](https://join.aub.edu.lb/apply/) or via [Common App](https://www.commonapp.org/explore/american-university-beirut-aub/)

The American University of Beirut is a private, independent university founded in
1866 as the Syrian Protestant College, making it one of the oldest institutions of
higher learning in the Middle East. AUB operates under a charter from the State of
New York and is accredited by the Commission on Higher Education of the Middle
States Association of Colleges and Schools. The university is organized into six
faculties and serves approximately 8,000 students with around 800 faculty members.
All instruction is conducted in English. AUB's 73-acre campus overlooks the
Mediterranean Sea in the heart of Beirut.

#### Difference between application types

- **Early Merit** — Apply by October 31. Requires top 25% class rank and
  higher SAT thresholds (see table below). Decisions arrive by end of
  December. Early Merit recipients are automatically considered for the
  30%/25%/20% merit scholarships (Early Admission / Early Action / Early
  Decision respectively).
- **Regular** — The main application window (November – December 20).
  Standard SAT minimums apply. Decisions by end of March.
- **Spring** — For mid-year enrollment. Limited seats, same requirements as
  Regular. Not all programs accept Spring applicants.
- **Transfer** — For students who have completed university-level courses.
  Students with fewer than 54 credits must still submit SAT scores. Students
  with 24+ credits (junior transfer) are **waived from the SAT**. Course
  syllabi required for credit evaluation.

Grade 10 and Grade 11 may be waived from the SAT requirement. Junior transfer
students (24+ credits) are also waived.

#### High-school grades

- Official transcripts for Grades 10, 11, and 12 are required.
- Early Merit applicants must rank in the **top 25%** of their class.
- A recognized secondary school certificate is required (Lebanese
  Baccalaureate, IB, French Bac, A-Levels, US High School Diploma, etc.).
- An equivalence from the Lebanese Ministry of Education is needed for
  non-Lebanese curricula.
- Hard-copy transcripts and diploma must be submitted by early July before
  enrollment.

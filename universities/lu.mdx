# Lebanese University (LU)

## Faculty

LU is **Lebanon's only public university**, founded in 1951.

It enrols **~80,000 students** — by far the largest in the country.
The university spans 16 faculties and institutes, each with its own
dean and regional branches across Mount Lebanon, the North, the Bekaa,
and the South. Main campuses sit at Hadath (Rafic Hariri), Fanar, and
Tripoli, with faculty branches in every governorate.

LU follows the French BMD system: Licence (3 years), Master (2 years),
Doctorat (3 years). Engineering takes 5 years, Medicine 7, Pharmacy
5–6, and Dental Surgery 5.

Tuition is near-free for Lebanese students. The Faculty of Engineering
holds EUR-ACE accreditation from CTI; FP, FEBA, FI, FPed, and FTHM are
HCÉRES-accredited.

Per-program URLs cover 10 of the 17 academic units. Rows in the
remaining 7 tables (FS, FPH, FLHS, FPed, FTHM, ISS, HIAES) link to the
faculty landing page since LU does not expose individual program pages
for them.

## Application

### Application Types + Windows

LU operates a **two-track admission system** unique among Lebanese
universities:

- **Direct admission** — for non-selective faculties (Sciences,
  Letters, Economics, Education, Information, Tourism, etc.).
  Students register directly after Lebanese Baccalaureate results
  are published.
- **Concours (CEE — Competitive Entrance Exam)** — required for
  selective faculties: **Engineering, Medicine, Dentistry, Pharmacy,
  Architecture, Fine Arts, Public Health**, and other selective
  programs. Each faculty runs its own CEE with separate dates.

## Scholarships

### University-offered scholarships

As a public university with near-free tuition, LU does not operate
a traditional scholarship system. Financial support comes via:

- **Government subsidy** — the Lebanese state covers the vast
  majority of educational costs for all enrolled students.
- **Faculty-specific exemptions** — some faculties may offer fee
  waivers or reduced rates on a case-by-case basis (e.g., for
  students from low-income backgrounds).

> Specific merit or need-based programs within LU faculties are not
> centrally documented. Contact individual faculty deaneries.

## Requirements

### SAT

LU **does not require SAT** for admission. Lebanese applicants are
admitted based on the **Lebanese Baccalaureate** results (with
faculty-specific minimum streams and averages) plus the CEE for
selective faculties.

### Lebanese Baccalaureate

- **Required for all faculties.** The eligible Baccalaureate stream
  and minimum average vary by faculty (e.g., Sciences stream for
  Engineering; Life Sciences for Medicine; any stream for FLHS or
  FEBA depending on the program).
- Non-Lebanese curricula (IB, French Bac, A-Levels, etc.) must
  obtain an **equivalence certificate** from the Lebanese Ministry of
  Education and Higher Education (MEHE) before applying.

### Competitive Entrance Exam (CEE)

Required for: Engineering, Medicine, Dentistry, Pharmacy, Fine Arts
& Architecture, Public Health, Agronomy/Veterinary Medicine, and
some others. Exam content varies by faculty:

- **Engineering** — Mathematics, Physics, and language test (FR / EN)
- **Medicine, Dentistry, Pharmacy** — Biology, Chemistry, Mathematics
  (taken after completing the first year at FS with ≥12/20 average)
- **Architecture, Fine Arts** — Drawing, design portfolio, and
  general aptitude
- **Public Health** — depends on track; usually Biology, Chemistry,
  general aptitude

### Language proficiency

Language requirements vary by faculty and branch:

- **Faculty of Engineering** — French and English (both required)
- **Faculty of Pharmacy** — has parallel French and English sections
- **FS, FEBA, FFAA** — primarily French/English with Arabic for
  general courses
- **FLHS, FLPAS, FPed, ISS** — primarily Arabic with French
  electives; FLHS English/French/Arabic departments teach in their
  respective languages
- **FA, FPH** — French primarily, with English-language tracks
  available

There is no centralized TOEFL/IELTS/DELF threshold — each faculty
sets its own language requirements via internal placement tests.

# Lebanese University (LU)

## Overview

- **Founded:** 1951
- **Type:** Public (the only state-funded public university in Lebanon)
- **Education Type:** French BMD system (Bachelor–Master–Doctorate, 3+2+3 years)
- **Campus(es):** Multiple campuses — Rafic Hariri Campus (Hadath), Fanar Campus, North Campus (Tripoli), Roumieh, and faculty branches across all governorates
- **Languages of instruction:** Arabic, French, English (varies by faculty and branch)
- **Websites:**
  - Main: [ul.edu.lb](https://www.ul.edu.lb/en)
  - Admissions: [ul.edu.lb/admission](https://ul.edu.lb/en/admission)
  - Faculties: [ul.edu.lb/faculties](https://ul.edu.lb/en/colleges-faculties-homepage)
  - Contact: [ul.edu.lb/contact-us](https://ul.edu.lb/en/contact-us)

The Lebanese University is Lebanon's only public institution of higher education, established
on December 3, 1951 following a student-led campaign for accessible education. It is the
largest university in Lebanon with approximately 80,000 students and over 5,400 academic
staff members. LU is organized into 16 faculties and institutes, each with its own dean,
branches, and admissions process. Unlike private Lebanese universities that follow the
American credit system, LU follows the French BMD system (Licence–Master–Doctorat).
Tuition is nearly free for Lebanese students, with only nominal registration and social
security fees. The Faculty of Engineering holds EUR-ACE accreditation from the Commission
des Titres d'Ingénieur (CTI), and the university is evaluated by Hcéres (French higher
education evaluation agency).

## Majors

LU is organized into 16 independent **faculties and institutes**, each operating its own
branches across Lebanon. The university offers 86 bachelor degrees across 77 tracks,
plus master's and doctoral programs. Degrees follow the BMD system (Bachelor: 3 years /
6 semesters, Master: 2 years, Doctorate: 3 years).

## Application

### Application types

LU admissions differ fundamentally from private universities. There are two paths:

- **Direct admission** — For faculties that accept students based on their Lebanese
  Baccalaureate results (e.g., Sciences, Letters, Economics, Education). Students
  register directly after results are published.
- **Concours (CEE — Competitive Entrance Exam)** — Required for selective faculties:
  Engineering, Medicine, Dentistry, Pharmacy, Architecture, Fine Arts, and some others.
  Each faculty runs its own concours with separate dates and requirements.

### Requirements

#### Lebanese Baccalaureate

- Required for all faculties. The eligible Baccalaureate stream and minimum average
  vary by faculty (e.g., Sciences stream for Engineering, Life Sciences for Medicine).
- Non-Lebanese curricula (IB, French Bac, A-Levels, etc.) must obtain an **equivalence
  certificate** from the Lebanese Ministry of Education and Higher Education (MEHE)
  before applying.

#### Concours (CEE)

- Required for: Engineering, Medicine, Dentistry, Pharmacy, Fine Arts & Architecture,
  and other selective faculties.
- Exam content varies by faculty (math/physics for Engineering, biology/chemistry for
  Medicine, drawing/design for Architecture, etc.).
- The Faculty of Engineering CEE covers material from the Lebanese Baccalaureate and
  French Baccalaureate programs. Language of instruction: French and English.

#### Language

Language requirements depend on the faculty and branch. The Faculty of Engineering
operates in French and English. Most humanities faculties teach primarily in Arabic
and French. Some science branches offer English-track options.

### University-offered scholarships

As a public university with near-free tuition, LU does not operate a traditional
scholarship system like private universities. Financial support is primarily through:

- **Government subsidy** — The state covers the vast majority of educational costs
  for all enrolled students.
- **Faculty-specific exemptions** — Some faculties may offer fee waivers or reduced
  rates on a case-by-case basis.

# Lebanese International University (LIU)

## Faculty

LIU offers 38 undergraduate programs across five schools, delivered on
nine campuses in Lebanon (Beirut, Bekaa, Saida, Nabatieh, Mount Lebanon,
Tripoli, Tyre, Rayak, and Halba-Akkar) plus three international campuses
(Yemen, Senegal, Mauritania). Each table lists the program, degree
type, home department, credits, and duration (years).

## Application

### Application Types + Windows

LIU operates on **rolling admissions** with three semesters per year
(Fall, Spring, Summer). Registration windows are published on the
[academic calendar](https://liu.edu.lb/LIU/calendar.php). The School of
Pharmacy has a separate application window, typically May through
mid-August.

| Term | Registration opens | Classes begin | Classes end | Final exams | Reference |
| ---- | ------------------ | ------------- | ----------- | ----------- | --------- |
| Fall 2025-26 | Aug 11, 2025 | Sep 29, 2025 | Jan 8, 2026 | Jan 12–24, 2026 | [Academic Calendar](https://liu.edu.lb/LIU/calendar.php) |
| Spring 2025-26 | Jan 9, 2026 | Feb 16, 2026 | Jun 4, 2026 | Jun 8–20, 2026 | [Academic Calendar](https://liu.edu.lb/LIU/calendar.php) |
| Summer 2025-26 | Jun 15, 2026 | Jun 29, 2026 | Jul 30, 2026 | Aug 3–7, 2026 | [Academic Calendar](https://liu.edu.lb/LIU/calendar.php) |
| Pre-Fall 2026-27 | May 16, 2025 (announced) | TBD | TBD | TBD | [LIU Pre-Fall 2026 Announcement](https://www.facebook.com/lebintuni/posts/get-ready-liu-studentspre-fall-2026-registration-opens-may-16-2025-at-1000-ampla/997462032590233/) |

**Difference between tracks:**

- **Fall** — main intake; full program availability across all five schools.
- **Spring** — mid-year intake; some majors may not accept new students.
- **Summer** — short term, typically 8 credits maximum; mostly used to
  catch up on courses, freshmen rarely admitted.
- **Pre-Fall (Pharmacy)** — separate window for the School of Pharmacy
  (May to mid-August), reflecting its distinct academic structure.

Reference: [LIU Admission & Registration](https://liu.edu.lb/NewLIU2022/AR/index.php) |

## Scholarships

### University-offered scholarships

LIU does not publish a catalog of named scholarship programs. The
financial-aid page describes a single discretionary aid package
reviewed by the Financial Aid Committee on a case-by-case basis.

#### Need-based aid

| Aid program | Coverage | Eligibility | Conditions | Reference |
| ----------- | -------- | ----------- | ---------- | --------- |
| **General Financial Aid** | Not published (committee-set per case) | Demonstrated financial need; ≥70% cumulative GPA after 30 credits; ≥67% of attempted credits passed (Satisfactory Academic Progress); enrolled in the minimum credits required per semester; not receiving an outside scholarship ≥50% of tuition | Annual renewal required; aid is reviewed yearly | [LIU Financial Aid](https://liu.edu.lb/LIU/view_page_content.php?page_name=RmluYW5jaWFsIEFpZA%3D%3D&template_id=Mw%3D%3D) |
| **School of Pharmacy** | **Not eligible** for financial aid (per LIU policy, due to the program's high operational cost) | — | — | [LIU Financial Aid](https://liu.edu.lb/LIU/view_page_content.php?page_name=RmluYW5jaWFsIEFpZA%3D%3D&template_id=Mw%3D%3D) |

**How to apply for financial aid:**

1. Pick up the Financial Aid Application form at the Students' Affairs
   Office (SAO) on any campus.
2. Schedule an interview with a Financial Aid Officer (FAO) at the SAO.
3. Submit the completed form with supporting documents **before the
   end of the 5th week of the semester**.
4. Renew every academic year using the Renewal Form before the
   published deadline.

Reference: [LIU Financial Aid](https://liu.edu.lb/NewLIU2022/admission/financialaid.php)

## Requirements

### SAT

LIU does **not** require SAT scores for admission. Admission is based
on the Lebanese Baccalaureate (or equivalent) and university placement
tests (UPTs).

### High-school grades

- A recognized secondary school certificate is required: Lebanese
  Baccalaureate II (any strand), Technical Baccalaureate (BT3), or an
  equivalent foreign credential as recognized by the Lebanese Ministry
  of Education and Higher Education.
- School of Education applicants must have an overall average of at
  least **50%** on their Baccalaureate II or third-year secondary
  exams.
- An equivalence from the Lebanese Ministry of Education is needed for
  non-Lebanese curricula.

Reference: [LIU Admission Requirements](https://liu.edu.lb/LIU/view_page_content.php?page_name=QWRtaXNzaW9uIFJlcXVpcmVtZW50cw%3D%3D&template_id=Mw%3D%3D)

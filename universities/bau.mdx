# Beirut Arab University (BAU)

## Overview

- **Founded:** 1960
- **Type:** Private
- **Education Type:** Mixed (English-medium for most faculties; Arabic and French in some courses within Human Sciences and Law)
- **Campus(es):** Beirut (Tarik El Jadida — main campus), Debbieh (est. 2006), Tripoli (est. 2010), Bekaa (est. 2011)
- **Language of instruction:** English (primary); Arabic and French in select faculties
- **Websites:**
  - Main: [bau.edu.lb](https://www.bau.edu.lb/)
  - Admissions: [bau.edu.lb/Admissions](https://www.bau.edu.lb/Admissions)
  - Financial Aid: [bau.edu.lb/Undergraduate/Financial-Aid-and-Scholarships](https://www.bau.edu.lb/Undergraduate/Financial-Aid-and-Scholarships)

Beirut Arab University was founded in 1960 by the Lebanese Waqf El-Bir wal Ihsan
Society with academic and logistical support from Egypt during the era of President
Gamal Abdel Nasser. BAU has grown from its original two faculties (Arts and Law)
to encompass 10 faculties across four campuses, offering 37 undergraduate and 131
postgraduate programmes to over 16,000 students. The university holds international
institutional accreditation from FIBAA (Germany) and is a member of multiple
international university associations. Notable alumni include former Lebanese Prime
Minister Rafic Hariri.

#### Explanation of application types

- **Freshman** — For students who have completed Grade 12 and hold a high school
  diploma. Must secure permission from the Equivalence Committee of the Lebanese
  Ministry of Education. SAT I required with a minimum of **950 (sciences)** or
  **870 (arts)**.
- **Sophomore** — For students holding a Lebanese Baccalaureate (any stream),
  Technical Baccalaureate, French Baccalaureate, or equivalent recognized
  certificate.
- **Transfer** — For students transferring from another university with
  completed credits and certified transcripts.
- **Entrance Exams** — Each major has a specific entrance exam that may include
  thinking skills questions, scientific subjects, personal interview, or
  drawing tests (for architecture/design).

Reference: [BAU Admissions](https://www.bau.edu.lb/Admissions/ProspectiveStudents)

- Certified copies of official certificates or diplomas when available.

Reference: [BAU Admissions](https://www.bau.edu.lb/Admissions/ProspectiveStudents)

#### English proficiency

All undergraduate applicants must pass BAU's English Language Admission Exam
with a minimum score of **60%**. Exemptions are granted if the applicant has
one of the following:

| Test | Minimum Score | Notes | Reference |
| ---- | ------------- | ----- | --------- |
| TOEFL (paper-based) | 500 | BAU TOEFL code: B516; institutional TOEFL not accepted | [BAU Admissions](https://www.bau.edu.lb/Admissions/ProspectiveStudents) |
| IELTS Academic | 6.5 | — | [BAU Admissions](https://www.bau.edu.lb/Admissions/ProspectiveStudents) |
| BAU English Entrance Exam | 60% | Administered at BAU | [BAU Admissions](https://www.bau.edu.lb/Admissions/ProspectiveStudents) |
| GCE (IGCSE) | Completed program | Fully exempted from BAU English exam | [BAU Admissions](https://www.bau.edu.lb/Admissions/ProspectiveStudents) |

Transfer students from English-medium institutions (2+ years) are also exempt.

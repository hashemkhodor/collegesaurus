# University of Balamand (UOB)

## Overview

- **Founded:** 1988
- **Type:** Private university affiliated with the Greek Orthodox Church of Antioch (operates as quasi-secular)
- **Education Type:** American liberal education model; some programs in French (via ALBA)
- **Campus(es):**
  - **Main campus:** Balamand, El-Koura (North Lebanon, adjacent to Balamand Monastery)
  - **St. George Health Complex:** Achrafieh, Beirut (medicine, health sciences)
  - **Sin El Fil, Beirut:** ALBA — Lebanese Academy of Fine Arts (French-track fine arts)
  - **Souk El Gharb, Aley:** Arts & Social Sciences, Business, Health, Library, Sciences
  - **Beino, Akkar (North Lebanon):** Issam M. Fares Faculty of Technology
- **Languages of instruction:** Arabic, English, French, and Spanish (varies by faculty)
- **Websites:**
  - Main: [balamand.edu.lb](https://www.balamand.edu.lb/)
  - Admissions Guide: [2025-2026 Admission Guide (PDF)](https://www.balamand.org/Style%20Library/PDFs/ProspectiveStudents/AdmissionGuide.pdf)

The University of Balamand was founded in 1988 by Orthodox Patriarch Ignatius IV of
Antioch. It is a private university affiliated with the Greek Orthodox Church of Antioch
but operates in a quasi-secular manner. UOB serves approximately **5,600–5,700 students**
across 4–5 campuses with **10 faculties and 1 institute**. It offers 162 majors total:
1 university diploma, 13 teaching diplomas, 57 undergraduate majors, 56 graduate majors,
1 MD, 1 PhD, and 29 medical specialty programs. The Académie Libanaise des Beaux-Arts
(ALBA), founded 1944, is Lebanon's first national institution for higher education in
Fine Arts and joined UOB in 1988.

## Majors

UOB offers 162 programs across 10 faculties and 1 institute spread across 5 campuses.
Programs follow the American credit system (~30 credits/year). Most bachelor programs
are **3–4 years**; architecture/engineering is **5 years**; medicine is **7 years**.
Year estimates below are derived from credit norms.

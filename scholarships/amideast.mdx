# AMIDEAST Scholarships (Lebanon)

:::danger US Funding Disruptions (2025-2026)

In February 2025, the Trump administration froze all State Department grant disbursements, disrupting Bureau of Educational and Cultural Affairs (ECA) exchange programs including YES, Fulbright, and MEPI-funded initiatives administered by AMIDEAST. While some funding was partially restored by late 2025, several programs remain affected. The **YES Program recruitment for 2026-2027 has been paused** ([source](https://www.yesprograms.org/countries/lebanon)), and the **MEPI Student Leaders Program summer 2025 was canceled** ([source](https://mepi.state.gov/student-leaders-program/)). Check each program's official page for the latest status before applying.

References: [NAFSA ECA Funding Freeze tracker](https://www.nafsa.org/ECAFundingFreeze) | [NPR: USAID shuts down](https://www.npr.org/2025/07/01/nx-s1-5451372/usaid-officially-shuts-down-and-merges-remaining-operations-with-state-department)

:::

## Overview

- **Provider:** [AMIDEAST](https://www.amideast.org/lebanon) (America-Mideast Educational and Training Services, Inc.)
- **Type:** US nonprofit educational organization; administers US government-funded exchange programs and its own privately funded scholarship programs in Lebanon
- **Reach:** Over 33,000 individuals per year in Lebanon ([source](https://www.amideast.org/lebanon))
- **Description:** AMIDEAST manages several scholarship and exchange programs across four levels: high school, undergraduate, graduate, and professional. Some are US government-funded (Fulbright, MEPI Tomorrow's Leaders, YES), while others are privately funded (Hope Fund, MSSF). This page covers AMIDEAST's **directly managed programs in Lebanon**, excluding Fulbright and MEPI Tomorrow's Leaders which have separate pages on this site.

The programs covered below are:

## YES Program (Youth Exchange & Study)

### Overview

The YES Program is a US State Department-funded cultural exchange that sends high school students from Lebanon to spend one academic year with an American host family, attending a US high school. Nearly **685 Lebanese alumni** have participated to date ([source](https://www.amideast.org/lebanon/find-a-scholarship/high-school-and-younger/yes-program-for-lebanon)).

:::warning

**Recruitment for the YES Program for academic year 2026-2027 has been paused.** Check [yesprograms.org](https://www.yesprograms.org/) or [yes-abroad.org](https://yes-abroad.org/) for updates on future programming ([source](https://www.amideast.org/lebanon/find-a-scholarship/high-school-and-younger/yes-program-for-lebanon)).

:::

### Grade & background requirements

- **Citizenship:** Lebanese citizen or long-term Palestinian resident in Lebanon; **not** a US citizen or green card holder ([source](https://www.amideast.org/lebanon/find-a-scholarship/high-school-and-younger/yes-program-for-lebanon))
- **Academic level:** Currently enrolled in Brevet or 1st Secondary (Lebanese Baccalaureate), or 9th-10th grade
- **GPA:** Minimum **B average (12/20)** for the past three years with no failing grades
- **English:** Minimum **B in English** for the past three years
- **Other:** Must demonstrate maturity, flexibility, and willingness to participate in host family daily life; agree to maintain at least C average (10/20) in a US high school; no prior semester or year at a US high school

## MENA Scholarship Support Fund (MSSF)

### Overview

The MSSF enables highly qualified students from underserved communities in the MENA region to pursue **undergraduate study in the United States**. The fund closes the gap between a student's financial aid package from a US university and their actual cost of attendance ([source](https://www.amideast.org/about-the-mssf)).

> **Note:** Palestinian students from Lebanon are directed to apply to the [Hope Fund](#hope-fund) instead ([source](https://www.amideast.org/our-work/find-a-scholarship/undergraduate-study/mssf/apply/apply)).

### Grade & background requirements

- **Citizenship:** MENA nationals; US citizens and green card holders are **not** eligible ([source](https://www.amideast.org/our-work/find-a-scholarship/undergraduate-study/mssf/apply/apply))
- **Academic level:** Must have secured admission with a substantial financial aid package to a 4-year undergraduate program at a US college or university
- **Financial need:** Cannot afford to study in the US without supplemental scholarship support
- **GPA:** Not publicly specified
- **Other:** Must be committed to pursuing a Bachelor's degree in the US

## Hope Fund

### Overview

Founded in 2000 by Dr. Fahim Qubain and his wife Nancy, the Hope Fund helps academically talented **Palestinian youth -- many from refugee backgrounds -- earn undergraduate degrees at US universities**. The program became part of AMIDEAST in May 2015. Nearly **200 Palestinian students** have benefited over nearly two decades ([source](https://www.amideast.org/our-work/find-a-scholarship/undergraduate-study/hope-fund/about-the-hope-fund/about-the-hope-fund)).

**Funding:** Entirely by private donations; **does not receive US government funding** ([source](https://www.amideast.org/our-work/find-a-scholarship/undergraduate-study/hope-fund/about-the-hope-fund/about-the-hope-fund)).

### Grade & background requirements

- **Nationality:** Palestinian refugee residing in Lebanon ([source](https://www.amideast.org/lebanon/find-a-scholarship/undergraduate-study/hope-fund))
- **Academic level:** Currently in **10th or 11th grade**
- **GPA:** Minimum **16/20** in each of the last three academic years
- **Financial need:** Cannot afford US study without a scholarship
- **Extracurriculars:** Active in research and activities such as sports, community projects, and arts
- **Exclusion:** US citizens and green card holders are not eligible

### Supported universities

US colleges and universities where the student secures admission with substantial financial aid. Roanoke College was an early partner providing full scholarships ([source](https://www.amideast.org/our-work/find-a-scholarship/undergraduate-study/hope-fund/about-the-hope-fund/about-the-hope-fund)).

### Benefits

- Covers supplemental educational costs **beyond** university aid and family contributions ([source](https://www.amideast.org/our-work/find-a-scholarship/undergraduate-study/hope-fund/about-the-hope-fund/about-the-hope-fund)):
  - Tuition gap
  - Books
  - Health insurance

  - Incidental expenses
- Application and testing fee support (SAT, TOEFL)
- Essay writing assistance

### Required documents

- School transcripts (past 3 years)
- Copy of official Brevet exam
- Copy of ID
- Certificates of awards / community service
- SAT & TOEFL scores (if available)

## Safar-Ibiary Syrian College Prep Program

### Overview

A **two-year preparatory program** that helps talented Syrian students living in Lebanon strengthen their university applications and win scholarships to US universities ([source](https://www.amideast.org/lebanon/find-a-scholarship/undergraduate-study/the-safar-ibiary-syrian-college-prep-program)).

### Grade & background requirements

- **Nationality:** Syrian living in Lebanon ([source](https://www.amideast.org/lebanon/find-a-scholarship/undergraduate-study/the-safar-ibiary-syrian-college-prep-program))
- **Academic level:** Currently enrolled in **grade 10 or 11**
- **GPA:** Minimum **16/20 (or 80/100)** in each of the last three academic years ([source](https://daleel-madani.org/civil-society-directory/amideast/calls/safar-ibiary-syrian-college-prep-program-amideast))
- **Financial need:** Cannot afford US study without a scholarship
- **Extracurriculars:** Active in research and activities (sports, community projects, arts)
- **Commitment:** Must be committed to pursuing a Bachelor's degree in the US
- **Exclusion:** US citizens and green card holders are not eligible

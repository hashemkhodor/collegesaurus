# Makhzoumi Foundation — Vocational Training & Education Programs

## Overview
- **Provider:** [Makhzoumi Foundation](https://makhzoumi-foundation.org/), founded in 1997 by HE. Deputy Fouad Makhzoumi; currently chaired by Mrs. May Naamani Makhzoumi ([source](https://makhzoumi-foundation.org/who-we-are/))
- **Type:** Private Lebanese non-profit NGO
- **Description:** The Makhzoumi Foundation is a national non-profit organisation whose mission is to "mobilize resources, build partnerships, and develop the capacities of communities while promoting targeted education, affordable healthcare, workable startups, sustainable development and secured livelihoods" ([source](https://makhzoumi-foundation.org/who-we-are/)). Its **Vocational Training Program** is one of five pillars (alongside Healthcare, Micro Credit, Environment & Development, and Relief Projects). Rather than offering traditional university scholarships, the Foundation provides **free and low-cost vocational and technical training courses** that lead to certificates or diplomas, with the goal of preparing trainees for the labour market ([source](https://makhzoumi-foundation.org/vocational-training/)).

## Grade & background requirements
- **Academic level:** No university degree required. Programs are designed for school leavers, dropouts, and anyone seeking vocational skills ([source](https://makhzoumi-foundation.org/vocational-training/)).
- **Age:** Some free programs (e.g. construction trades in partnership with GIZ) target youth aged **16 to 30** ([source](https://makhzoumi-foundation.org/building-skills-build-future/)).
- **Nationality / residency:** Programs are open to Lebanese residents. Several projects funded by international partners (UNHCR, GIZ, French Development Agency) also serve refugees and displaced persons ([source](https://makhzoumi-foundation.org/building-skills-build-future/)).
- **Financial need:** Many courses are offered free of charge, particularly those funded by international development partners. Some professional courses may carry nominal fees.
- **Other:** No standardised GPA or test-score requirements. Applicants are assessed based on the specific program's prerequisites.

## Application window

Courses run on a **rolling basis** throughout the year, with new cohorts starting regularly at each training centre. There is no single annual deadline.

| Stage              | Typical date                                                                 |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Applications open  | Rolling — check the [vocational training page](https://makhzoumi-foundation.org/vocational-training/) or the Foundation's mobile app for current offerings |
| Applications close | Varies per course and centre                                                 |
| Interviews         | Some programs include an initial assessment at the branch                    |
| Decisions          | Typically communicated within days of applying                               |

To apply, visit the nearest branch office or use the [Makhzoumi Foundation mobile app](https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=org.makhzoumifoundation.general) to browse training tracks and schedules ([source](https://makhzoumi-foundation.org/vocational-training/)).

## Supported universities

The Makhzoumi Foundation is **not a university scholarship provider**. It operates its own training centres rather than funding study at universities. However, the Foundation participates in education fairs (e.g. Academia Lebanon) to guide youth toward higher-education pathways and offers language, computer-skills, and aptitude-test preparation that can help students bridge into university programs ([source](https://makhzoumi-foundation.org/academia-lebanon/)).

## Benefits

The Foundation's vocational training programs offer the following:

- **Tuition:** Many courses are **free of charge**, especially those co-funded by international partners such as GIZ, the French Development Agency, and UNHCR ([source](https://makhzoumi-foundation.org/building-skills-build-future/)).
- **Course categories:** Digital Literacy & IT Skills, Languages (English, French, Arabic), Beauty Academy, Business & Enterprise Skills, Soft Skills, Electronics, Photography, Cell Phone Repair, Nursing-related specialties, Construction Trades (wall painting, tiling), Sewing & Embroidery, Accounting, Welding, Home Electrician, Elderly Care, and Culinary Arts ([source](https://makhzoumi-foundation.org/vocational-training/)).
- **Industry certifications:** The Foundation is an authorised **Cisco Networking Academy** and **Microsoft Imagine Academy** partner, allowing trainees to earn internationally recognised IT certifications ([source](https://makhzoumi-foundation.org/new-creative-information-technology-step-lebanese-youth/)).
- **Diplomas & certificates:** Courses can lead to a certificate or diploma recognised on the local labour market ([source](https://makhzoumi-foundation.org/vocational-training/)).
- **Toolkits:** Graduates of certain trades (e.g. construction) receive a toolkit to help them start working immediately ([source](https://makhzoumi-foundation.org/building-skills-build-future/)).
- **Internships:** The **Step Forward Program** places trainees in internship and skill-based volunteering opportunities with employers ([source](https://makhzoumi-foundation.org/vocational-training/)).
- **Career guidance:** The Foundation offers the [Josour Platform](https://makhzoumi-foundation.org/), a complimentary skills-analysis and career-alignment tool.

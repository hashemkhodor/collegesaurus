# Unite Lebanon Youth Project (ULYP)

## Overview
- **Provider:** [Unite Lebanon Youth Project (ULYP)](https://www.ulyp.org/)
- **Type:** NGO (nonprofit, registered as NGO #134 in Lebanon)
- **Founded:** 2010
- **Description:** ULYP empowers marginalized children, youth, and women living in Lebanon
  with the skills and knowledge they need to become active agents of change, without any
  discrimination. Through its flagship **Bridge Program**, ULYP assists capable secondary
  school students in Lebanon to prepare for university, secure funding for undergraduate
  degrees at Lebanese and international universities, and transition into the workforce.
  Over **900 students** have benefited from the Bridge Program, with **850+ graduates**
  located across 46+ countries and an **80%+ employment or further-education rate** among
  alumni.
  ([ULYP Bridge Program](https://www.ulyp.org/projects/view/27) |
  [Meem Foundation](https://www.meemfoundation.org/grants-awarded/unite-lebanon-youth-project-ulyp-bridge-program/) |
  [AUB LEAD](https://boldly.aub.edu.lb/impact-stories/the-unite-lebanon-youth-project-joins-aubs-lead-initiative/))

## Grade & background requirements
- **Academic level:** Secondary school students (grade 10-12) applying for undergraduate scholarships
  ([ULYP Bridge](https://www.ulyp.org/projects/view/27))
- **Baccalaureate / official exam score:** Must score above **72%** on official exams
  ([Refugee Education Fund](https://refugee-educationfund.org/en/access-to-higher-education-ulyp/))
- **Nationality / residency:** Lebanese, Palestinian, and Syrian students **residing in Lebanon**
  ([AUB LEAD](https://boldly.aub.edu.lb/impact-stories/the-unite-lebanon-youth-project-joins-aubs-lead-initiative/))
- **Financial need:** Yes — the program targets **underprivileged and marginalized youth**;
  applicants must meet means-testing criteria
  ([Refugee Education Fund](https://refugee-educationfund.org/en/tag/ulyp/))
- **Selection process:** Two-phase selection starting in 10th grade — top achievers join ULYP,
  then go through interviews to join the Bridge Program
  ([Refugee Education Fund](https://refugee-educationfund.org/en/access-to-higher-education-ulyp/))

## Benefits
The Bridge Program provides comprehensive support that extends **5-6 years** from secondary school through university graduation
([ULYP Bridge](https://www.ulyp.org/projects/view/27)):

- **Tuition:** Full tuition scholarships for undergraduate degrees, funded through university
  partnerships, private donors, and organizations including USAID, Mastercard Foundation,
  Al Ghurair Foundation, and Asfari Foundation
  ([ULYP News](https://unitelebanonyouth.wordpress.com/tag/scholarships/))
- **Living expenses:** Funding to cover living and other costs
  ([AUB LEAD](https://boldly.aub.edu.lb/impact-stories/the-unite-lebanon-youth-project-joins-aubs-lead-initiative/))
- **Test preparation:** University Preparatory Course (UPC) covering SAT, TOEFL, and IELTS
  preparation for ~150 students annually
  ([ULYP Bridge](https://www.ulyp.org/projects/view/27))
- **University counseling:** Application assistance, career counseling, and guidance for ~350
  scholarship applicants per year
  ([ULYP Bridge](https://www.ulyp.org/projects/view/27))
- **Academic & psychosocial support:** Ongoing academic mentoring and psychosocial services
  throughout university
  ([AUB LEAD](https://boldly.aub.edu.lb/impact-stories/the-unite-lebanon-youth-project-joins-aubs-lead-initiative/))
- **Civic engagement & leadership training:** Workshops and activities to develop leadership skills
  ([AUB LEAD](https://boldly.aub.edu.lb/impact-stories/the-unite-lebanon-youth-project-joins-aubs-lead-initiative/))
- **Career readiness:** Workplace transition assistance and career readiness skills training
  ([Refugee Education Fund](https://refugee-educationfund.org/en/access-to-higher-education-ulyp/))

ULYP awards approximately **100 scholarships per year**
([ULYP Bridge](https://www.ulyp.org/projects/view/27)).

# Kamal A. Shair Fund

## Overview
- **Provider:** Estate and legacy of Dr. Kamal A. Shair (1930--2008), co-founder of [Dar Al-Handasah (Shair & Partners)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dar_Al-Handasah)
- **Type:** Endowed faculty chairs, research laboratory, and research fund at AUB -- **not a traditional student-application scholarship**
- **Description:** Dr. Kamal A. Shair's philanthropy at AUB takes the form of endowed institutional assets rather than a direct student scholarship fund. His contributions established a world-class research laboratory, an endowed chair in leadership, and a research endowment that indirectly benefits graduate students through funded research positions and access to advanced instrumentation. Students do not "apply" to a Kamal Shair scholarship; instead, they benefit by working in the facilities and programs his endowments sustain. ([Kamal A. Shair Memorial](https://www.kamalshair.info/), [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kamal_Al-Shair))

## Grade & background requirements
This is **not a scholarship students can apply for directly**. There is no GPA threshold, nationality requirement, or application form associated with the Kamal A. Shair endowments.

Graduate students benefit indirectly by:
- Using KAS CRSL equipment and training for thesis research (open to all AUB graduate students and faculty)
- Securing research assistantships on projects funded through the CRSL Endowed Research Fund
- Enrolling in programs supported by the endowed chairs (e.g., leadership courses at OSB)

## Supported universities

## Benefits
Because this is not a direct student scholarship, there are no tuition waivers or stipends disbursed to students under the "Kamal A. Shair Fund" name. The indirect benefits to students include:

- **Research infrastructure:** Free access to 40+ advanced instruments at the KAS CRSL for thesis and coursework ([KAS CRSL](https://www.aub.edu.lb/fas/crsl/Pages/default.aspx))
- **Training:** Hands-on instrument training sessions provided by CRSL staff ([KAS CRSL](https://www.aub.edu.lb/fas/crsl/Pages/default.aspx))
- **Research assistantships:** Graduate students may be hired on faculty projects funded by the CRSL Endowed Research Fund
- **Publications:** Access to CRSL facilities contributes to ~250 publications annually, boosting student research profiles

### About Dr. Kamal A. Shair
Dr. Kamal A. Shair (10 August 1930 -- 21 August 2008) was born in As-Salt, Jordan. He studied at AUB starting in 1945, then earned a BS and MS in Engineering from the University of Michigan (1949--1950) and a Doctor of Engineering from Yale University (1954--1955). He served as a chemical engineering faculty member at AUB from 1956 to 1962 and co-founded Dar Al-Handasah in 1956 with four AUB colleagues, growing it into one of the largest engineering consultancies in the Arab world. He served as an AUB trustee from 1990 until his death and received an honorary doctorate from AUB in 2003. His autobiography, *Out of the Middle East*, was published in 2006. ([Kamal A. Shair Memorial](https://www.kamalshair.info/), [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kamal_Al-Shair), [Encyclopedia.com](https://www.encyclopedia.com/international/encyclopedias-almanacs-transcripts-and-maps/shair-kamal-1930))

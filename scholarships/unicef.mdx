# UNICEF Education Programs (Lebanon)

## Overview
- **Provider:** [UNICEF Lebanon](https://www.unicef.org/lebanon/)
- **Type:** International NGO / UN agency
- **Description:** Unlike traditional university scholarships, UNICEF Lebanon focuses on
  **basic and secondary education** for vulnerable children and youth. Through partnerships
  with the Ministry of Education and Higher Education (MEHE), UNICEF supports access to
  public schooling, runs non-formal education pathways, and operates community learning
  centres. These programs primarily serve out-of-school children, Syrian and Palestinian
  refugees, and economically disadvantaged Lebanese students at the **pre-university** level.
  ([Source](https://www.unicef.org/lebanon/education-programme))

:::caution Not a university scholarship
UNICEF Lebanon does not offer tuition grants for university students. This page is included
because UNICEF's programs are a major part of the Lebanese education landscape and may
benefit younger siblings or families of university-age students. If you are looking for
**university-level** funding, see other pages in this section.
:::

## Programs at a glance

### 1. Transition Resilience Education Fund (TREF)
Launched in June 2022 by MEHE in partnership with UNICEF, the EU, and Germany (via KfW),
TREF is the main funding mechanism for Lebanon's public schools.
([Source](https://www.unicef.org/lebanon/press-releases/ministry-education-launches-transition-resilience-education-fund-support-lebanons))

- **What it funds:** Teacher salaries (special-contract teachers), school operating costs,
  and learning supplies for **all** children enrolled in public schools regardless of
  nationality.
- **Scale:** Over **USD 85 million annually** disbursed to support MEHE programs; funds go
  directly to **1,074+ public schools** benefiting an estimated **1,200+ schools** and
  **12,000 teachers**.
  ([Source](https://www.unicef.org/lebanon/transition-and-resilience-education-fund-strengthening-lebanons-public-education-system))
- **Who benefits:** All children in Lebanese public schools (Lebanese and non-Lebanese).

### 2. Non-Formal Education (NFE) Pathways
UNICEF delivers non-formal education programs targeting children and youth who have been
**out of school** for extended periods, providing structured learning that can serve as a
bridge back into the formal school system.
([Source](https://www.unicef.org/lebanon/stories/unicefs-non-formal-education-programme-equipping-children-lebanon-skills-succeed))

- **Target group:** Out-of-school children and adolescents, including refugees.
- **Subjects:** Literacy, numeracy, life skills, and catch-up curricula aligned with the
  Lebanese formal education standards.
- **Pathway:** Designed as a stepping stone back into formal public school enrollment.

### 3. Makani Community Centres
"Makani" (Arabic for "My Space") centres are multi-service community hubs operated by
UNICEF with implementing partners such as Amel Association International.
([Source](https://www.unicef.org/lebanon/stories/makani-safe-space-learning-and-hope))

- **Services:** Non-formal education, psychosocial support, child protection, life-skills
  training, health and hygiene awareness, and recreational activities.
- **Scale:** Over **50 centres** operating across Lebanon.
- **Who can attend:** Vulnerable children, youth, and families — particularly those affected
  by displacement and crisis.

### 4. Learning Passport (Digital Platform)
A UNICEF-developed digital learning platform, powered by Microsoft Community Training,
providing free online and offline educational content.
([Source](https://www.unicef.org/documents/learning-passport-and-non-formal-education-vulnerable-children-and-youth-lebanon))

- **Website:** [lebanon.learningpassport.org](https://lebanon.learningpassport.org/)
- **Content:** Early childhood education, primary and secondary curricula, adolescent
  skills, and technical/vocational education modules.
- **Access:** Free; works both online and via **offline hub devices** deployed in areas with
  limited connectivity.

### 5. Teaching and Learning Innovation Fund (TLIF)
Launched in October 2025, funded by the European Union and implemented by UNICEF, TLIF
empowers public schools to design and lead their own innovative solutions to improve
teaching and learning.
([Source](https://www.unicef.org/lebanon/press-releases/ministry-education-and-higher-education-launches-teaching-and-learning-innovation))

## Grade & background requirements
- **Academic level:** Pre-primary through secondary (not university-level)
- **GPA:** No GPA requirement — programs target vulnerable and out-of-school populations
- **Nationality / residency:** Lebanese citizens, Syrian refugees, Palestinian refugees,
  and other non-Lebanese children residing in Lebanon
- **Financial need:** Programs are designed for economically disadvantaged populations;
  public school support benefits all enrolled students

## Application window
UNICEF education programs in Lebanon do **not** follow a single application cycle. Access
depends on the specific program:

| Program | How to access |
| ------- | ------------- |
| TREF (public school support) | Enroll in any Lebanese public school — support is automatic |
| Non-Formal Education | Referral through UNICEF partner NGOs or MEHE |
| Makani Centres | Walk in to any [Makani centre](https://www.unicef.org/lebanon/stories/makani-safe-space-learning-and-hope) |
| Learning Passport | Register free at [lebanon.learningpassport.org](https://lebanon.learningpassport.org/) |

## Supported universities
UNICEF Lebanon's education programs **do not cover university tuition**. They focus on
basic and secondary education. For university-level scholarships, see other pages in this
section such as [USAID USP](/scholarships/usaid-usp) or [Fulbright](/scholarships/fulbright).

## Benefits
- **Tuition (public school):** UNICEF subsidizes enrollment fees so public school
  attendance is free or near-free for all enrolled children
  ([Source](https://www.unicef.org/lebanon/education-programme))
- **Learning supplies:** Provided to all students in supported public schools
- **Teacher salaries:** Funded through TREF to keep schools staffed and operational
- **Non-formal education:** Free structured learning programs for out-of-school children
- **Psychosocial support:** Available through Makani centres at no cost
- **Digital learning:** Free access via the Learning Passport platform
